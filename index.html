📊 Metrics Interpretation Guide

Latency (ms / sec):
Lower is better. Measures how fast the model generates output. Fast models are preferred for real-time applications.

CLIP Score:
Higher is better. Measures how well the generated image/video matches the input prompt. Range: 0 → 1 → Closer to 1 = better semantic alignment.

FID (Fréchet Inception Distance):
Lower is better. Measures similarity between generated images and real images. → Lower FID = more realistic outputs.

SSIM (Structural Similarity Index):
Higher is better. Measures structural similarity between images. Range: 0 → 1 → Closer to 1 = better quality and structure preservation.

Aesthetic Score:
Higher is better. Measures visual appeal and artistic quality. → Higher score = more visually pleasing output.

FVD (Fréchet Video Distance):
Lower is better. Video equivalent of FID. → Lower FVD = more realistic and coherent videos.

Motion Score:
Higher is better. Measures smoothness and realism of motion in videos. → Higher = better motion consistency.

Consistency:
Higher is better. Measures temporal consistency (frame-to-frame stability). → Important for avoiding flickering or identity changes.
